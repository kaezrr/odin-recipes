Bengali Mutton Biriyani

Description

Calcuttans are truly passionate about their mutton biryani. Kolkata has its distinct style of biryani which is on a par with the best biryanis in India—including Lucknow-style biryani and the Hyderabadi biryani. Kolkata biryani, in spite of the usage of a staggering number of spices, is not spicy.

It is subtle and fragrant without being dry. A good Kolkata biryani should be moist and can be eaten on its own—no raita or salan required. This style of biryani is descended from the Lucknow style biryani that came to Calcutta with the exiled Nawab of Awadh, Wajid Ali Shah (remember Satyajit Ray’s “Satranj Ke Khilari”?).

Ingredients

  • Mutton
  • Basmati Rice
  • Onion
  • Biriyani Masala
  • Potato
  • Ghee

Steps

  1. Fry potatoes
  2. Make birista
  3. Make Biriyani masala
  4. Marinate mutton
  5. Soak rice
  6. Cook mutton
  7. Boil rice
  8. Assemble all Ingredients
  9. Dum cook for 30 minutes
